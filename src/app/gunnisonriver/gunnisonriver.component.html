GUNNISON RIVER MAPS

The Gunnison River forms at the confluence of the East River and Taylor River near the town of Almont. From Almont it flows 23 miles to Blue Mesa Reservoir. This uppermost section of the Gunnison holds plenty of wild brown and rainbow trout. It can be waded or floated if the flow is sufficient. There is some private water here that can only be accessed by boat. Below the outlet of Blue Mesa, the Gunnison feeds two more reservoirs (Morrow Point and Crystal) before entering the Black Canyon. Just above the canyon is East Portal, a much easier access point to the Gunnison. Getting into the Black Canyon will require a steep hike, but the fishing is well worth it. Plenty of brown and rainbow trout live in the canyon and are not as pressured as the trout in many of Colorado's other popular rivers. The Gunnison River in Black Canyon has a prolific salmonfly hatch (a large stonefly) in late spring. Trout will eat salmonflies off the surface with reckless abandon and can provide an angler with some of the best dry fly fishing anywhere.


Below the Black Canyon is the more accessible Gunnison Gorge. The quality of the fishing here is also excellent. The Gunnison can hold up to 10,000 trout per mile in the Black Canyon and downstream through Gunnison Gorge. The river holds trout for many more miles, including downstream of the town of Delta.
