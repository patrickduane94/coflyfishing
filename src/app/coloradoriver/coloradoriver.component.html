COLORADO RIVER MAPS

The Colorado River begins in Rocky Mountain National Park and flows west through the mountains to Utah. The Colorado has many miles of public access and is renowned for its excellent population of wild brown trout. The upper river from Granby to Parshall has plenty of pull offs along Route 40. The river here has a good mix of riffles, runs, and longer holes. Expect mostly brown trout and some rainbows in the 12-18" range. Just downstream of Kremmling the Colorado enters Gore Canyon, well known for its class V whitewater. The Colorado through Glenwood Canyon is known for its scenery and steep canyon walls which run right along I-70. The river holds trout for most of its length in Colorado, even well past Glenwood Springs. The lower river is fished best from a boat because of the size but does hold some big fish. Due to the excellent public access on this river, anglers have no problem finding solitude with a short walk from one of the many access points on the Colorado.
